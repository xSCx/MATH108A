 -(1)  $A_1=\mathbb R^3$, since $\,\vec v_1,\,\vec v_2,\,\vec v_3$ are linearly independent.
\vskip.1in
$A_2$ is the solid pyramidal cone with vertex the origin $(0,0,0)$ and edges given by the positive multiples of $\,\vec v_1,\,\vec v_2,\,\vec v_3$ resp.
\vskip.1in
$A_3$ the solid pyramid with vertices $\,\vec v_1,\,\vec v_2,\,\vec v_3$ and $(0,0,0)$.
\vskip.1in
$A_4$ the triangle in $\mathbb R^3$ with vertices $\,\vec v_1,\,\vec v_2,\,\vec v_3$.
\vskip.1in
$A_5$ the plane passing along the points $\,\vec v_1,\,\vec v_2,\,\vec v_3$.

 -(2) Assume that 
$$
\cup_{k=1}^n V_k
$$
is a sub-space and that if there does not exist $j_0\in\{1,2,..,n\}$ such that
$$
\cup_{k=1}^n V_k=V_{j_0}.
$$ 

\vskip.1in
Without lost of generality we can assume that for any $j\in \{1,..,n\}$ 

$$
V_j\not\subset V_1\cup..\cup V_{j-1}\cup V_{j+1}\cup..\cup V_n.
$$

Hence, there exists 

$$
\vec x_1\in V_1,\;\;\;\;\;\vec x_1\notin V_2\cup....\cup  V_n
$$

 and there exists  
 
 $$\vec x_2\in V_2,\;\;\;\;\;\vec x_2\notin V_1 \cup V_3\cup..\cup V_n.$$


Consider the sequence of vectors

$$
\vec v_m = \vec x_1 + m\vec x_2,\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;m \in\mathbb N.
$$

Since 
$$
\vec v_m \in \cup_{k=1}^n V_k,\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\forall \,m\in \mathbb N,
$$

there 
exists $j_0\in\{1,..,n\}$ and $m_1,\,m_2\in \mathbb N$ with $m_1\neq m_2$ such that

$$
\vec v_{m_1}= \vec x_1 + m_1\vec x_2 \in V_{j_0},\;\;\;\;\;\;\,\vec v_{m_2}= \vec x_1 + m_2\vec x_2 \in V_{j_0}.
$$

Therefore, 

$$
x_1,\,x_2\in  V_{j_0}.
$$

which yields a contradiction.
